Week-by-Week Syllabus Breakdown

Detailed curriculum for each track — what you learn every week in the internship

🛠️

Tools Used: ANSYS Fluent, ANSYS Meshing, ICEM CFD, OpenFOAM, CFD-Post, ParaView, Python (post-processing)

Weeks 1–2

CFD Fundamentals

  • Governing equations: Navier-Stokes, continuity, energy
  • Types of fluid flow: laminar, turbulent, compressible
  • Introduction to ANSYS Workbench environment
  • Case study: flow over a flat plate
Weeks 3–4

Geometry & Mesh Generation

  • CAD import and geometry cleanup in ANSYS SpaceClaim
  • Structured vs unstructured meshing
  • Boundary layer meshing (inflation layers)
  • Mesh quality metrics and refinement
Weeks 5–6

ANSYS Fluent — Solver Setup

  • Pressure-based vs density-based solvers
  • Turbulence models: k-ε, k-ω SST, Spalart-Allmaras
  • Boundary conditions: inlet, outlet, wall, symmetry
  • Steady-state simulation of airfoil flow
Weeks 7–8

Advanced CFD Analysis

  • Transient (unsteady) flow simulation
  • Heat transfer: conjugate heat transfer, thermal BCs
  • External aerodynamics: drag and lift of 3D bodies
  • Convergence monitoring and residual analysis
Weeks 9–10

OpenFOAM & Post-Processing

  • OpenFOAM case structure and directory setup
  • Running simpleFoam and rhoCentralFoam cases
  • ParaView visualization: velocity, pressure, streamlines
  • Comparative study: ANSYS vs OpenFOAM results
Weeks 11–12

Major Project & Evaluation

  • Independent CFD project: student-selected aerospace application
  • Project report writing aligned with UGC-AICTE norms
  • Research paper guidance (journal/conference submission)
  • Project viva and final evaluation
🛠️

Rocket Fundamentals

Aerodynamics & Stability

Propulsion & Trajectory

  • Solid propellant motors: thrust curve, Isp, total impulse
  • Motor selection for target altitude and payload
  • Trajectory simulation in MATLAB/Simulink
  • Recovery system design: parachute sizing
Week 4 / Weeks 5–12

Structural Analysis & Project

  • Airframe loads and buckling analysis
  • 3D CAD modelling in SolidWorks
  • Full rocket design project and documentation
  • For 12-week: advanced propulsion, multi-stage design, research paper

FEA Theory & Introduction

Meshing & Material Models

Structural Analysis

Dynamic & Thermal Analysis

Composite & Advanced Topics

  • Composite material layup in ANSYS ACP
  • CFRP analysis: ply failure, delamination
  • Topology optimization for lightweight aerospace parts
  • HyperMesh for complex meshing workflows

Frequently Asked Questions

What software tools are taught in the MARS Exploration CFD internship? +

The CFD internship covers ANSYS Fluent, ANSYS Meshing, OpenFOAM, ICEM CFD, CFD-Post, and ParaView. Students also learn mesh generation, turbulence modelling (k-ε, k-ω SST), heat transfer simulation, and basic Python scripting for post-processing. All topics include hands-on simulation exercises.

Which software is used in the Rocket Design internship? +

The Rocket Design internship uses OpenRocket, RASAero II, NASA CEA (for propulsion thermodynamics), MATLAB/Simulink for trajectory simulation, SolidWorks or CATIA for 3D CAD modelling, and Microsoft Missile DATCOM for aerodynamic coefficient estimation.

What is the difference between the CFD internship and FEA internship? +

CFD (Computational Fluid Dynamics) focuses on fluid flow, aerodynamics, and heat transfer simulation using ANSYS Fluent and OpenFOAM. FEA (Finite Element Analysis) focuses on structural integrity, stress, fatigue, and deformation analysis using ANSYS Mechanical and SolidWorks Simulation. Both are critical aerospace engineering disciplines and are offered as separate 12-week tracks.
