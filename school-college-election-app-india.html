How to Conduct a School Election – Step by Step

1

Teacher Downloads the App

Install EVM Voting Machine Mock Poll from Google Play Store on your personal Android phone (or the school's device). It's free and downloads in seconds.

2

Announce Candidates Beforehand

Hold nominations in class a day before. Collect candidate names and (optionally) photos. Prepare the list so setup is fast on election day.

3

Add Candidates in the App

Enter each candidate's name. Upload their photo from the gallery or choose from 60+ emoji symbols. Set the election title (e.g., "Class Monitor – Class 9B").

4

Set the Result Password

Choose a private password — don't share it with students. This ensures no student can peek at results while voting is still in progress.

5

Call Students One by One

Call each student to the front desk. Hand them the phone. They press their chosen candidate's button privately — the beep confirms the vote. Take back the phone.

6

Announce Results Dramatically

Enter your password to stop voting. The bar chart results appear — show the screen to the whole class. Announce the winner. Share results on the class WhatsApp group.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can a teacher on any Android phone use this app? +
Yes. The app works on any Android phone (Redmi, Samsung, Realme, OPPO, Vivo, POCO, OnePlus, Motorola, etc.) running Android 5.0 or above. No special device is needed. It has been verified working on Redmi 9 with Android 10 — a common teacher smartphone in India.
How many students can vote using this app? +
There is no limit on voters. Each student votes one at a time on the phone. Whether you have 30 students in a classroom or 300 students in a college auditorium, the app handles it. The only limit is 12 candidates per election — more than sufficient for any school or college post.
Can I run elections for multiple posts on the same day? +
Yes. Simply run multiple separate voting sessions — one for President, one for Vice-President, one for Secretary, etc. Each session is set up with its own candidates and has its own results. Share each result before starting the next session.
Is the voting private? Can classmates see who a student voted for? +
Yes, voting is completely private. Each student votes alone while holding the phone — no one can see the screen from behind. The app does not record who voted for whom — only the total count per candidate is tracked. There is no voter-to-vote traceability whatsoever.
Does the school need a special phone or tablet? +
No. The teacher's own personal Android phone is sufficient. A tablet also works. For Bluetooth Controller Mode, you need two Android devices — the teacher's phone and any second Android device (even an old one works as the EVM).
Can students access or tamper with results during voting? +
No. Results are locked behind the election officer's (teacher's) password. Students only see the ballot screen when voting. They cannot access any running count or results. Only after all students have voted and the teacher enters the password do the results appear.
