What is the MARS Exploration Aerospace Internship Program?

Credit-Wise Learning Outcomes (UGC–AICTE Guidelines)

4-Credit Internship (4 Weeks) — Skill Familiarization & Application

The 4-week program is ideal for early-stage learners seeking short-term, credit-based exposure. After successful completion, students will be able to:

  • Understand fundamental aerospace engineering concepts in their chosen domain (Rocket Propulsion / Drone Technology / RLV / Aerospace Vehicle Design)
  • Apply basic design, analysis, and simulation tools used in the aerospace industry
  • Interpret engineering drawings, models, and technical data
  • Perform introductory hands-on tasks aligned with real-world aerospace problems
  • Demonstrate awareness of industry standards, safety norms, and best practices

6-Credit Internship (12 Weeks) — Advanced Skill Development & Research Orientation

The 12-week program is designed for in-depth technical learning and research readiness. After successful completion, students will be able to:

  • Develop advanced technical knowledge in their chosen aerospace specialisation (FEA / Design & Analysis / Rocket Propulsion / Drone Technology / CFD)
  • Independently perform design, modelling, simulation, and performance evaluation tasks
  • Solve complex engineering problems using analytical and computational methods
  • Execute a major industry-oriented or academic project with proper methodology
  • Use professional aerospace tools and software following industry workflows
  • Analyze results, validate designs, and propose engineering improvements
  • Demonstrate research aptitude — including literature review and technical writing
  • Prepare a comprehensive project report aligned with UGC–AICTE evaluation norms
  • Present technical outcomes confidently in reviews, viva, and assessments
  • Exhibit professional ethics, teamwork, and industry readiness

⚠️ Mandatory: The 6-credit (12-week) internship includes mandatory project evaluation and review for credit certification.
