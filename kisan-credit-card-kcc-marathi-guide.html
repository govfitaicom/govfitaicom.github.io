Frequently Asked Questions

What is the interest rate on KCC loan? +
The effective interest rate on KCC is only 4% per annum after government interest subvention of 2%. Without subvention it is 7%, but eligible farmers who repay on time get 3% additional rebate, making the effective rate just 4%.
How to apply for Kisan Credit Card? +
Visit your nearest SBI, Punjab National Bank, Bank of India, or cooperative bank. Fill the KCC application form. Submit Aadhaar, 7/12 land record, 2 passport photos, and bank account details. KCC is issued within 14 working days. PM-KISAN beneficiaries get priority processing.
Can fishermen and livestock farmers get KCC? +
Yes! Since 2020, the KCC scheme has been extended to fish farmers, marine fishers, inland fishers, and animal husbandry workers (dairy, poultry). The same 4% interest rate and ₹3 lakh limit applies.
What can I use KCC funds for? +
KCC funds can be used for crop cultivation expenses (seeds, fertilisers, pesticides, labour), post-harvest expenses, maintenance of farm equipment, allied activities including animal husbandry and fisheries, and consumption requirements of the farmer household.
What is the KCC credit limit? +
The KCC limit is based on your land holding, crop pattern, and scale of finance. The maximum limit is ₹3 lakh for short-term credit requirements. For term loans and allied activities, the limit can be higher based on the specific purpose.
