Banking Sector in India - Overview

The banking sector in India is one of the largest employers, offering stable careers with excellent growth opportunities. With over 1.5 lakh bank branches across the country, banks recruit thousands of Probationary Officers (PO), Clerks, and Specialist Officers every year. Banking jobs are highly sought after due to competitive salaries, job security, and prestigious work profiles.

India has 12 public sector banks (after recent mergers), 22 private sector banks, 46 foreign banks, and numerous cooperative banks. The Institute of Banking Personnel Selection (IBPS) conducts Common Recruitment Process (CRP) for recruitment in public sector banks, making it the largest banking recruitment body in India.

Major Banking Examinations

1. IBPS PO (Probationary Officer)

IBPS PO is one of the most prestigious banking examinations. Probationary Officers are recruited at Scale-I Officer cadre and handle customer relations, loan processing, branch operations, and team management. After training, POs are posted as Assistant Managers.

Preparation Strategy

6 Month Study Plan:

Months 1-2: Foundation

  • Complete basic mathematics (Class 10 level)
  • Learn fundamental reasoning concepts
  • Build English grammar foundation
  • Start reading business newspapers (Economic Times, Business Standard)

Months 3-4: Advanced Preparation

  • Practice 50+ puzzles and seating arrangements
  • Master data interpretation and analysis
  • Solve previous year question papers (last 5 years)
  • Learn shortcut techniques for quantitative aptitude

Months 5-6: Mock Tests & Revision

  • Take 40-50 full-length mock tests
  • Analyze each test thoroughly
  • Work on weak areas
  • Daily current affairs revision
  • Speed and accuracy improvement

Best Books for Bank Exams:
