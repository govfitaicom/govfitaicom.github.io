Why Is Your Check Engine Light (CEL) On?

The orange or yellow Check Engine Light (CEL) — also called the Malfunction Indicator Lamp (MIL) — is one of the most misunderstood warning lights on an Indian car's dashboard. Most drivers either ignore it out of fear of a ₹5,000+ workshop bill, or panic and drive straight to a service centre only to be told it's a minor sensor issue.

The truth is: the CEL simply means your car's ECU (Engine Control Unit) has detected a sensor reading outside its normal range and stored a Diagnostic Trouble Code (DTC). That code could mean something as minor as a loose fuel cap — or as serious as a failing catalytic converter.

What Are OBD2 DTC Fault Codes? A Simple Explanation

Every OBD2-compliant vehicle stores Diagnostic Trouble Codes (DTCs) when a sensor or system reports a fault. These codes follow a universal 5-character format:

P System: P=Powertrain, B=Body, C=Chassis, U=Network
0 Type: 0=Generic OBD2, 1/2/3=Manufacturer-specific
4 Subsystem: 0=Fuel/Air, 1-2=Fuel/Injector, 3=Ignition, 4=Emission, 5=Speed/Idle, 6=ECU, 7-8=Transmission
20 Specific fault number (00-99)
Example: P0420 = Powertrain / Generic / Emission Control / Catalyst Efficiency Below Threshold

The Car Kundali car diagnostic app automatically decodes every DTC into plain English — so instead of seeing "P0420", you see "Catalyst System Efficiency Below Threshold (Bank 1) — possible causes: faulty catalytic converter, failing O₂ sensor, exhaust leak."

Key Things to Check Before Buying

  • Bluetooth, not Wi-Fi: Car Kundali (like most Android OBD2 apps) requires a Bluetooth ELM327 adapter. Wi-Fi adapters are primarily for iOS and don't pair reliably with Android.
  • Version: Look for ELM327 v1.5 or v2.1 (not v2.2+ clones which often have bugs). The version matters more than the price point for genuine chips.
  • Protocol support: Should support ISO 15765-4 (CAN), ISO 14230-4 (KWP2000), ISO 9141-2, and SAE J1850 — covering all Indian market cars.
  • Power LED: A quality adapter has a solid power/activity LED. Cheap ones flicker or don't light up consistently.

Understanding Live Engine Data — What Car Kundali Shows You

Beyond just reading fault codes, Car Kundali functions as a full real-time vehicle diagnostic tool for Android. Here's what each live data parameter means for your car's health:

RPM

Engine RPM

Idle should be 650–900 RPM. Erratic idle or RPM hunting = throttle body, idle air valve, or vacuum leak issue.

ECT

Engine Coolant Temp

Normal operating: 85–105°C. Consistently over 110°C = investigate cooling system, thermostat, or head gasket.

MAF

Mass Airflow (g/s)

At idle: 2–7 g/s typical. Abnormally low = dirty MAF sensor. Higher than expected = air leak.

STFT

Short Term Fuel Trim

Should be near 0%. Values above +15% = running lean (check MAF, O₂ sensor). Below -15% = running rich.

O2

Oxygen Sensor Voltage

Should oscillate 0.1–0.9V rapidly. Slow switching or flat line = failing O₂ sensor. Affects fuel economy.

BATT

Battery Voltage

Ignition ON (engine off): ~12.4–12.6V. Engine running: 13.8–14.8V. Below 13V running = alternator issue.

TPS

Throttle Position

Idle: 10–20%. Full throttle: ~80–100%. Sticky or erratic TPS can cause hesitation and misfire DTCs.

MAP

Manifold Absolute Pressure

At idle: 25–45 kPa (vacuum). At WOT: near atmospheric (100 kPa). Used instead of MAF on speed-density engines.

IAT

Intake Air Temperature

Should be 5–10°C above ambient. Very high IAT in Indian summers (50°C+) can cause knock retard and power loss.
