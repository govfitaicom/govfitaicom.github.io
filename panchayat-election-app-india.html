How This App Helps Voter Education in Rural India

The SVEEP Connection — Systematic Voters' Education

The Election Commission of India's SVEEP (Systematic Voters' Education and Electoral Participation) programme runs awareness camps across rural India before every panchayat election. The EVM Mock Poll app is the perfect tool for these camps — it lets first-time voters, especially women and youth, experience the EVM voting process before they enter the real polling booth.

Many voters in remote villages have never seen an EVM before. When they arrive at the polling booth for the first time, they feel nervous and confused. A 5-minute demo session with this app — conducted at the aanganwadi, school, or gram sabha — means every voter arrives at the booth confident and ready.

Teaching Panchayati Raj in Schools

Class 6, 7, and 8 social science curricula across India include chapters on panchayati raj and local self-government. Teachers can use the EVM Mock Poll app to turn theory into practice — run a live mock sarpanch election in the classroom. Students vote exactly as they would in a real panchayat election, learning democracy by doing it.

Select Hindi Language

Open the app and switch to Hindi. All candidate names, instructions, and buttons will appear in Hindi — comfortable for every village voter and panchayat official.

3

Add Candidates & Symbols

Enter candidate names (up to 12). Add each candidate's photo or choose an emoji symbol. Set the election title — e.g. "Sarpanch Chunav Demo 2026" or "Ward Panch Election".

4

Set Password (Election Officer Only)

Only the election officer or panchayat secretary sets the result password. This is known to no one else — results remain locked until all voters have voted.

5

Call each voter to the phone one at a time. They press the button next to their candidate — a beep confirms the vote. Hand the phone back and call the next voter.

6

Announce Results

After all votes are cast, the election officer enters the password. The bar chart result appears — vote count per candidate, winner highlighted. Share to the village WhatsApp group.
