About Public Sector Undertakings (PSUs)

Public Sector Undertakings (PSUs) are government-owned corporations that operate in various sectors like oil & gas, power, steel, coal, telecommunications, and banking. India has over 250 Central PSUs and numerous state PSUs. These companies combine the stability of government jobs with competitive private sector salaries, making PSU jobs highly desirable among engineering graduates.

PSU jobs offer the best of both worlds - attractive compensation packages comparable to private sector MNCs, job security like government services, excellent work-life balance, comprehensive benefits, and opportunities to work on nation-building projects. Many PSUs like ONGC, NTPC, IOCL, and BHEL are listed among Fortune 500 companies and offer world-class work environments.

Recruitment Through GATE

The Graduate Aptitude Test in Engineering (GATE) is the primary gateway to PSU jobs for engineering graduates. Over 50 PSUs recruit through GATE scores, making it one of the most important exams for engineers. GATE is conducted by IITs/IISc and tests candidates on their technical knowledge and problem-solving abilities.

Eligibility Criteria for PSU Jobs

Educational Qualification:

  • B.E./B.Tech in relevant engineering discipline
  • Minimum 60% marks (55% for SC/ST in most PSUs)
  • GATE score in corresponding paper
  • Some PSUs accept only current year GATE score, others accept 2-3 year old scores

Age Limit:

  • Generally 18-30 years (relaxation for reserved categories)
  • Upper age limit varies by PSU (some allow up to 32 years)
  • Age relaxation: OBC - 3 years, SC/ST - 5 years, PWD - 10 years

Selection Process

Typical PSU Recruitment Process:

  1. GATE Score Shortlisting: PSUs announce cutoff percentile/score
  2. Application: Eligible candidates apply online
  3. Written Test: Some PSUs conduct additional technical/aptitude test
  4. Group Discussion: Conducted by some PSUs like ONGC, GAIL
  5. Personal Interview: Technical and HR interview
  6. Medical Examination: Fitness test as per company standards
  7. Document Verification: Educational certificates, caste certificate
  8. Final Merit List: Based on GATE score + interview performance

GATE Score Weightage:

Different PSUs use different formulas:

  • ONGC: GATE score + Interview (ratio varies)
  • IOCL: Only GATE score for shortlisting, final selection through interview
  • NTPC: Written test + Interview
  • GAIL: GATE score + GD + Interview

GATE Preparation Strategy for PSU Jobs

Understand GATE Syllabus:

  • GATE tests engineering fundamentals from B.Tech curriculum
  • Each paper has core subjects + General Aptitude
  • Focus on all subjects equally as questions can come from anywhere

Study Plan (8-10 Months):

Phase 1: Foundation (Months 1-4)

  • Complete all core subjects from standard textbooks
  • Make notes for quick revision
  • Solve examples from each chapter
  • Focus on understanding concepts, not memorization

Phase 2: Problem Solving (Months 5-7)

  • Solve previous 15 years GATE papers
  • Practice numerical problems extensively
  • Identify weak areas and work on them
  • Join test series for practice

Phase 3: Revision & Mock Tests (Months 8-10)

  • Revise all subjects 2-3 times
  • Take 30-40 full-length mock tests
  • Analyze each test performance
  • Quick revision of formulas and concepts

Best Books by Subject (Mechanical Engineering Example):

  • Engineering Mechanics: SS Bhavikatti, Beer & Johnston
  • Strength of Materials: RK Bansal, Timoshenko
  • Thermodynamics: PK Nag, Yunus Cengel
  • Fluid Mechanics: RK Bansal, Frank White
  • Heat Transfer: PK Nag, Holman
  • Manufacturing: PC Sharma, Kalpakjian
  • Theory of Machines: SS Rattan, Khurmi & Gupta

PSU Interview Preparation

Technical Interview:

  • Be thorough with your engineering subjects
  • Prepare your B.Tech projects in detail
  • Know about the PSU - its operations, recent news, projects
  • Practice commonly asked technical questions
  • Be ready to solve problems on paper/board

HR Interview:

  • Why do you want to join this PSU?
  • Why should we select you?
  • Your strengths and weaknesses
  • Career goals (short-term and long-term)
  • Willingness to work in remote locations
  • Questions about your resume and achievements
