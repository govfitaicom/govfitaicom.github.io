How to Conduct a Society Election Using This App

Here is the complete step-by-step process for a housing society AGM election using the EVM Voting Machine Mock Poll app:

1

Before the AGM – Install & Prepare

Download the app on one (or two, for Bluetooth mode) Android phones. Collect candidate names and photos beforehand. Charge both phones to 100%.

2

Open App & Add Candidates

Enter each candidate's name. Upload their photo from the gallery or choose an emoji symbol. Set the election title (e.g., "Society Chairman Election 2026").

3

Set the Result Password

The election officer (society secretary) sets a private password. No one else should know this — it prevents early result peeking during voting.

4

Pair with Controller Phone (Optional)

For Bluetooth mode: pair the EVM phone with the Controller phone over Bluetooth. The EVM is passed to voters; the Controller stays with the election officer.

5

Begin Voting – One Member at a Time

Call each flat owner or member one by one. Hand them the EVM phone. They press their chosen candidate's button — a beep confirms their vote. Take back the phone.

6

Close Voting & Reveal Results

Once all members have voted, the election officer enters the password to stop voting. The results screen shows a live bar chart with vote counts and winner.

7

Share Results to Society WhatsApp Group

Tap Share and send the results image directly to your society WhatsApp group. Every member — present or absent — gets the result instantly.

✅ Pro Tip for Bluetooth Mode: Use the Bluetooth EVM + Controller setup for maximum credibility. The election officer keeps the Controller phone and can see who has voted. The EVM phone is passed to each voter for private, individual voting — exactly like a real polling booth.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the society election result legally valid? +
No. Results from the EVM Mock Poll app are for informational and mock purposes only and have no legal standing. For legally binding society elections, consult your state's cooperative housing society act and your RWA's bylaws. However, the app is ideal for informal consensus-building, mock votes, and preliminary elections where results are later formalised by the secretary in the minutes.
Can I conduct elections for multiple posts in one AGM? +
Yes. Simply run multiple separate voting sessions — one for Chairman, one for Secretary, one for Treasurer, etc. Each session is set up independently with different candidates. Results from each session can be shared separately to the WhatsApp group.
What if a flat owner is not physically present at the AGM? +
The app is designed for in-person voting where each voter is physically handed the phone. For proxy votes or absent-member voting, the election officer would need to handle that as per your society's proxy voting rules before the election session. The app itself supports only in-person sequential voting.
Can I add candidate photos instead of symbols? +
Yes. When adding each candidate, you can pick any photo from your Android phone's gallery as that candidate's symbol. This means voters can see the face of each resident they're voting for — eliminating confusion about candidate identity.
Does the app store voter names or flat numbers? +
No. The app does not collect, store, or transmit any personal data — not voter names, flat numbers, or individual vote choices. Only the total count per candidate is tracked. The vote is completely secret and anonymous. No data ever leaves the device.
Which phone brands work best for society elections? +
The app works on all major Android brands used in India — Redmi/Xiaomi, Samsung, Realme, OPPO, Vivo, POCO, OnePlus, Motorola. Any Android phone running Android 5.0 or above will work. For Bluetooth Controller Mode, Android 6.0+ is recommended on both phones. The app has been verified working on Redmi 9 with Android 10.
